Croissant

These flaky, crescent-shaped pastries are a delightful French creation. Layers of buttery dough are meticulously folded and baked to perfection, resulting in a golden exterior that shatters with each bite. Whether enjoyed plain or filled with chocolate, almond paste, or ham and cheese, croissants remain a symbol of indulgence and morning bliss.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Water
  • Whole Milk
  • Sugar
  • Unsalted Butter
  • Yeast
  • salt

Method to Prepare

  1. Make Croissant dough

    1. Knead for 3 minutes and store in fridge for 12 hours

  2. Laminate the dough

    1. Make butter slab and refrigerate till needed
    2. Roll dough disc into square
    3. Seal butter in dough
    4. Roll out to 20 cm x 60 cm and fold
    5. Refrigerate 30 minutes
    6. Rotate 90 degrees
    7. Roll out to 20 cm x 60 cm and fold
    8. Refrigerate 30 minutes
    9. Rotate 90 degrees
    10. Roll out to 20 cm x 60 cm and fold
    11. Refrigerate until day 3

  3. Dividing, shaping, proofing and baking

    1. Roll out to 20 cm x 110 cm - part 1
    2. Often needed! Take 20 min. fridge time if length not in one go
    3. Roll out to 20 cm x 110 cm - part 2
    4. Divide and shape the croissants
    5. First coat of egg wash
    6. Proof to perfection (indication 2 hours)
    7. Second coat of egg wash
    8. Bake for 15-18 minutes
    9. Ready!
